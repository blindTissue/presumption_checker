4. PRESUMPTION:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Does kidney cancer surgery always result in the removal of an entire kidney?

FACT-CHECK:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Here's a comprehensive fact-check:

1. Direct Answer:
No, kidney cancer surgery does NOT always result in the removal of an entire kidney.

2. Key Facts and Evidence:
- Partial nephrectomy (removing only the tumor and some surrounding tissue) is often possible
- Surgical approach depends on:
  * Tumor size
  * Location of tumor
  * Stage of cancer
  * Patient's overall health
- Partial kidney removal preserves kidney function and is preferred when feasible
- Techniques include:
  * Minimally invasive surgery
  * Robotic-assisted surgery
  * Traditional open surgery

3. Important Nuances/Context:
- Radical nephrectomy (full kidney removal) is sometimes necessary
- Factors determining surgical approach:
  * Tumor larger than 4 cm often requires full kidney removal
  * Tumors in complex locations might necessitate complete removal
  * Metastatic spread can influence surgical decision

4. Underlying Presumption:
The original presumption is INACCURATE. Kidney cancer surgery can often preserve kidney function through partial removal.

Recommendation: Patients should consult with urological oncology specialists to determine the most appropriate surgical approach for their specific case.
