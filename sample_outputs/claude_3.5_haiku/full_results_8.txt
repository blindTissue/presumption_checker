1. Direct Answer:
Yes, there are several alternative screening methods for colon cancer that are less invasive than traditional colonoscopy.

2. Key Facts and Evidence:

- Stool DNA Test (Cologuard): Checks for blood and DNA markers every 3 years
- CT Colonography (Virtual Colonoscopy): Uses CT scans to examine colon
- Flexible Sigmoidoscopy: Examines lower portion of colon every 5 years
- Blood-based screening tests are emerging but not yet widely recommended

3. Important Nuances:
- Less invasive tests may miss some potential issues
- Positive results on non-invasive tests typically require follow-up colonoscopy
- Different tests have varying sensitivity and specificity
- Screening recommendations vary by individual risk factors and age

4. Underlying Presumption Accuracy:
The presumption is accurate. Multiple alternative screening methods exist that are less invasive than traditional colonoscopy.

Recommendation: Consult healthcare professionals to determine the most appropriate screening method based on individual health profile and risk factors.
